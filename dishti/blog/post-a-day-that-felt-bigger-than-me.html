A Day That Felt Bigger Than Me

My first campus event and the feeling of stepping into something new.

Walking into that crowd, I felt both small and part of something important. It was the first time I understood belonging as an action, not just a feeling.

That day taught me how small gestures add up — a shared laugh, a nod, the hush before someone speaks. Each moment stacked into a sense of being held by something larger than myself.

Since then, I look for those small connections; they are the quiet architecture of belonging.
