The First Time I Stood and Spoke

My first presentation and the nerves I couldn’t hide.

That trembling at the start taught me how to hold presence. The talk was small, but its lessons stayed.

Since then, I try to prepare with curiosity rather than fear — to invite the audience into a conversation. Each talk becomes practice in listening as much as speaking.

Those early nerves remind me that vulnerability is often the bridge to clarity.
