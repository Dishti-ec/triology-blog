Two Degrees, One Brain

Engineering and English, and the chaos of choosing both.

Balancing two fields taught me how to shift lenses — how to think like an engineer and feel like a reader, sometimes at the same time.

That duality often felt messy but generative; technical training sharpened my clarity, while literature taught me to hold nuance. Together they expanded what I could imagine building.

Each project now benefits from both rigor and curiosity — a small practice I aim to keep.
