Manthan: The Debate & Elocution Experience

From an introvert to a confident speaker, Manthan was my leap into the unknown. This is a tale of courage, self-discovery, and the power of stepping out of your comfort zone.

Why I Chose Elocution

Manthan—the debate and elocution competition. I participated in elocution because I thought I didn’t know how to perform in a debate, but I really wanted to learn and see how debates actually take place. You can’t just enter to watch, so I took the chance and participated in elocution. It was a great opportunity to see, learn, and speak on my own.

Stepping Out of My Comfort Zone

I am a very introverted person, but for the past few years, I’ve been trying to come out of my comfort zone. This was another experience to challenge myself—to make my own voice stand out, to be confident, to get familiar with the stage, and to learn how to own it. I’ve always worked in teams and loved it, but voicing myself out is something I need to learn.

The Day of the Event

I kept telling myself: no matter what happens, I will still go on stage. There’s no option to go back; the only path is forward. Even though I wasn’t fully prepared, I went up, spoke, and—even though I fumbled—it was a great win for me. I believed in myself and completed what I started, where the old me might have quit.

Support & Growth

When I got down from the stage, I saw my real friends who stayed for me and encouraged me. I am happy that I did it. This was an attempt to make my own voice stand out, to be confident in myself, and to get familiar with the stage. Every challenge like this is a step toward becoming the person I want to be.
