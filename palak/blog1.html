The Event that really helped me open up

From a shy participant to someone who could own the stage, this event was my first step into a world of confidence and creativity. Discover how a single experience can change everything!

From Shy to Stage

A foundation course is usually a melting pot of students from every imaginable field, but for me, it was something much more personal. I walked into those first sessions with a clear goal: to experience something entirely new. I wanted to step out of my comfort zone, dive into different activities, and challenge myself to do things I had never tried before.

1. The Day I Found My Scream

The theater session was led by an instructor who was loud, energetic, and incredibly intense. During an exercise where we had to improvise a song on the spot, everyone was required to sing at least one line. I did my part, but I was so quiet that the instructor didn't even notice. When I told him I’d already sung, he looked at me and challenged: "Is that as loud as you can be?"

Up until that point, I felt like every move I made was calculated—almost as if I were restricted to performing in a certain, measured way. When I told him "yes," he didn't let it slide. He asked me to scream as high as I could, multiple times. In that moment, the "silent one" disappeared. I felt genuinely open, realized my words actually mattered, and gained a massive boost of confidence.

2. From Fear to Footwork: Classical Dance

While I’ve always loved dancing, I’ve often stayed away from certain styles out of a paralyzing fear of "messing up". This course pushed me to finally try Classical Dance. Instead of overthinking the technique, I just leaned into the movement. To my surprise, I didn’t just get through it—I did pretty well! I enjoyed it so much that I’m now considering pursuing it professionally. It turns out the only thing holding me back wasn't a lack of talent, but the fear of making a mistake.

3. Owning My Roots on the Runway

The final highlight was the fashion show. The theme was "Different Cultures", and I realized that if I didn’t step up to represent my own heritage, no one else in the room would. Stepping out there was a solo mission. I did it all on my own, and representing my culture with pride was the final piece of the puzzle. It taught me that I don't need to wait for someone else to lead the way; I can take the stage myself.

The Takeaway

  • Independence: Learning to go and do things on my own.
  • Confidence: Realizing I can be loud, graceful, and bold when I need to be.
  • Momentum: The courage to carry these lessons into all my upcoming ventures.

Sometimes, you have to stop calculating your next move and just do what feels right.
