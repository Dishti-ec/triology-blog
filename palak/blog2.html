E-Summit 2026: Mind the Product

A spontaneous "yes" led to an unforgettable journey at IIT Roorkee's flagship competition. From uncertainty to victory, this is a story of teamwork, suspense, and the thrill of winning against all odds.

The Call That Changed Everything

I got a call from my friend to participate in a competition held by IIT Roorkee. Without thinking much, I said yes. The goal for all of us was simple: learn how to work in a team and discover new things. The competition was the flagship event of E-Summit 2026, "Mind the Product"—a summit for entrepreneurs and aspiring entrepreneurs. We wanted to learn, refine our qualities, and just see how far we could go.

Unfolding Rounds & Unexpected News

The first two rounds were online, so we felt there was nothing to lose. We didn’t even think about the third round. If you got selected for the third, you were invited to attend the E-Summit—a three-day event at IIT Roorkee, with the final round held there. We just wanted to learn and maybe get a participation certificate.

When the day for the final result came, other teams from our university got calls that they were selected. We didn’t. We were told to wait until the end of Sunday. If we didn’t get a call, it meant we weren’t selected. Monday morning came—still nothing. We thought it was over. But then, another team got a call that morning. We contacted the E-Summit team, and they said, "You'll get a call if you're selected. There are many applicants, so it's taking time."

The Moment of Truth

By evening, I finally gave up the "want" of the result. The moment I stopped thinking about it and was ready to accept anything, I got the mail: "Congrats! Team Sheleads, you are selected!" It was crazy! We were going to IIT Roorkee!

The Journey & The Pitch

We contacted the other selected teams, discussed with mentors and teachers, and set off for Uttarakhand, Roorkee. We decided to prepare everything for the competition but also just enjoy the three-day event. On the day of the final pitching round, we were out there practicing. I got a bit nervous, but my teammates handled me so well. We went in, gave our pitch, and the judge was smiling by the end. He asked us two questions, and that was it—over in under 10 minutes!

We came out almost screaming, "It’s done!" It went really well. Three out of four of us thought we could win, but we still didn’t know. We went for lunch, and I finally got to relax—sat on a swing in the hostel garden, called my mom, and spent time with my dear friend Dishti.

Victory & Reflection

After 3-4 hours, it was time for the final result. I got a call: "We won!" What! We literally thought we were just going for a fun trip, but we won! We didn’t know the position yet, but it was top 3. I rushed to the venue, waited for our team’s name, and went up for the trophy!

I still remember working on the day of Uttarayan, when I was supposed to be on the terrace with my family and cousins. But I stayed downstairs and worked. This experience taught me the power of will and trust in ourselves. No matter where you come from, you can do it. The biggest lesson: keep your head up and give your best, without worrying about the outcome.
